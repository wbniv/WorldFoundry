## Classical MIDI Files: CC0 / CC BY Licensing Research

This report covers the major sources of classical MIDI files, with emphasis on which ones grant licenses that permit commercial use in a shipped game (i.e., CC0 or CC BY/CC BY-SA — no NC restriction).

---

### Key legal context first

A MIDI file has two potential copyright layers:

1. **The composition** — pre-1928 works (Bach, Beethoven, Mozart, etc.) are in the public domain everywhere. No composition copyright issue.
2. **The MIDI arrangement/performance itself** — this is a new creative work authored by whoever encoded the MIDI. It can be copyrighted regardless of how old the underlying piece is. This is the layer that matters for sourcing.

No MIDI file old enough to have expired naturally into the public domain yet exists — the format is too young. All MIDI files are either explicitly licensed or implicitly all-rights-reserved.

---

### Source-by-source findings

#### 1. OpenScore / Open Goldberg / Open Well-Tempered Clavier — BEST FOR BACH
**License: CC0 (explicit)**
**Commercial: Yes, no attribution required**

OpenScore is a joint MuseScore + IMSLP initiative to encode public-domain scores under CC0. All transcriptions are released under CC0. The most immediately useful for WF:

- **Open Goldberg Variations** (Bach, BWV 988) — CC0, MIDI downloadable from musescore.com
- **Open Well-Tempered Clavier** (Bach, BWV 846–893) — CC0, MIDI downloadable
- **OpenScore Lieder Corpus** — 1,300+ songs (German/French art song), CC0, also mirrored on GitHub at `github.com/OpenScore/Lieder`

MIDI export is available per-score via the MuseScore.com interface, or bulk-converted via MuseScore desktop's batch-export CLI. These are the cleanest, most legally unambiguous classical MIDI files available.

**Caveat:** Coverage skews heavily toward Bach (the two large collection efforts focused there). Mozart and Beethoven have fewer OpenScore editions; you'd have to check what's been tagged "OpenScore (CC0)" on musescore.com for each composer.

#### 2. Mutopia Project
**License: Mix — Public Domain, CC BY, CC BY-SA (some CC BY-NC, but those are opt-out)**
**Commercial: Yes for PD and CC BY / CC BY-SA pieces; No for CC BY-NC pieces**

Mutopia hosts 2,124 pieces encoded in LilyPond, with MIDI exported from the engraving source. Every piece lists its license explicitly. The project uses CC BY 4.0, CC BY-SA 4.0, and Public Domain as the "free culture" licenses recommended for new contributions, alongside older CC BY 3.0 and CC BY-SA 3.0 variants. Some older contributions use CC BY-NC licenses — those cannot be used commercially.

The MIDI files here are generated from LilyPond source so they are metronomic (no expressive timing), but they are legally clean and available in bulk from `www.mutopiaproject.org`. Filter by license when downloading — look for "Public Domain", "CC BY", or "CC BY-SA" tags and skip "CC BY-NC".

Composer coverage: Bach, Beethoven, Mozart, Haydn, Schubert, Brahms, Handel, and many others.

#### 3. Musopen
**License: Mix — CC0 for recordings Musopen commissioned; varies for user-uploaded content**
**Commercial: Yes for CC0-labeled items; verify per item**

Musopen's primary mission is audio recordings (MP3/FLAC), not MIDI. Their MIDI availability is limited — they do not appear to host a substantial MIDI collection distinct from sheet music PDFs. Their commissioned recordings (the "Musopen Recording Project" Kickstarter recordings, for example) are released CC0, but those are audio files.

For **sheet music**, Musopen hosts PDF scores under varying CC licenses. Some of those PDFs could be converted to MIDI via MuseScore, but the resulting MIDI would derive its license from the sheet music's stated license, which varies per item.

**Bottom line for WF:** Musopen is not a practical direct source of CC0 MIDI files. It is a good source of CC0 audio recordings if you ever want to ship Ogg tracks instead of MIDI-rendered playback.

#### 4. IMSLP
**License: Highly variable — CC BY, CC BY-NC, Public Domain, and everything in between**
**Commercial: Only CC BY (without NC) items**

IMSLP does host some MIDI files alongside its PDFs, with each file tagged with its individual license. CC BY 3.0 MIDI files exist (permitting commercial use with attribution); so do CC BY-NC variants (non-commercial only). IMSLP itself warns that it cannot verify the accuracy of copyright claims on uploaded files.

For commercial use: you must check the license tag on each individual MIDI file — there is no blanket license for IMSLP MIDI content. Given that OpenScore and Mutopia are more uniformly licensed, IMSLP MIDI is the higher-effort, higher-risk option for a game project. IMSLP is better used as a secondary check ("is this piece also available with a clean license elsewhere?").

#### 5. Kunstderfuge.com
**License: Non-commercial only (personal use); small CC BY-NC-SA subset**
**Commercial: No**

19,300+ classical MIDI files, the largest single collection, but the terms of service explicitly prohibit commercial use. ~13% (2,192 files) carry CC BY-NC-SA, which also prohibits commercial use. Not usable for WF without a separate per-file commercial license negotiated directly with the site. Skip.

#### 6. piano-midi.de (Bernd Krueger)
**License: CC BY-SA (Germany)**
**Commercial: Yes, with attribution + ShareAlike**

High-quality, expressive piano MIDI files for Bach, Beethoven, Brahms, Chopin, Debussy, Haydn, Mozart, Schubert, Schumann, Tchaikovsky, and others. The MIDI files are licensed CC BY-SA (German jurisdiction). This means:

- Commercial use is permitted
- Must credit "Bernd Krueger, http://www.piano-midi.de"
- Any adaptation must be distributed under the same CC BY-SA license

The ShareAlike clause is relevant: if WF ships derivative MIDI arrangements, those derivatives must also be CC BY-SA. Shipping the MIDI files as-is (unmodified, embedded in an IFF chunk) is fine — the game itself is not a derivative of the MIDI files.

Good source for expressive solo piano performances of major classical works. Not orchestral.

#### 7. Classical Archives
**License: Subscription-based; files are not freely licensed**
**Commercial: No (without paid license)**

Not usable as a free source. They now primarily operate as a paid streaming service. Archive.org has a mirrored dump of older Classical Archives MIDI content, but those files do not carry explicit CC licenses and their use would be legally ambiguous.

### Recommendation for WF

For the MIDI music subsystem described in `/home/will/WorldFoundry/docs/investigations/2026-04-14-audio-sound-music.md`:

**Primary source: OpenScore CC0 collection** — use for Bach (Goldberg, WTC, inventions). Zero legal friction, no attribution required, MIDI export available.

**Secondary source: Mutopia Project (CC BY / PD pieces)** — broader composer coverage. Filter at download time to exclude NC-tagged items. Attribution is simple (a text file in the game's credits or a `CREDITS.txt` next to the IFF assets).

**Tertiary: piano-midi.de** — if you want expressive piano MIDI rather than metronomic LilyPond output, Krueger's files are CC BY-SA and commercially usable. Attribute in credits; no viral effect on the engine or game code since MIDI files are data assets, not source.

**Avoid for commercial use:** Kunstderfuge (NC-only), Classical Archives (subscription), any IMSLP MIDI tagged CC BY-NC or CC BY-NC-SA.
